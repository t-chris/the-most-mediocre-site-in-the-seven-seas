One Coat

A great day to watch paint dry.

January 16th, 2019



One coat,
Two coat,
Three coat,
Four.

Is there a need to do any more?
But under watchful eyes
And cold-dry lips,
He stands alone, in the summer heat of June,
Whose predacity leaves no need to wonder why
A fifth one is needed.

Five coat
Six coat,
Seven coat,
Eight.

Doomed in the man sits fast this fate,
Viewing nothing but the superficial
Glistening, across Junoâ€™s Olympic rays
Though even he cannot
hide the other layers, the splinters,
perfect imperfections.

Nine coat.
Ten.

He was being asked to try again,
But in those imperfections remain
In the paint and in the sky,
And in the talks of TV sets.
A graceful prance, of springing curls,
And the gradual spitting of coated wood
Began as light revealed a final truth.
Reality could not hide again,
For another day in summer heat.

And the man could not paint away,
The cracks and curls upon that wall
The lies that kept contracts alive,
A smile that hid some gritting teeth,
The words that kept egos afloat,
For truth needs not another coat.
